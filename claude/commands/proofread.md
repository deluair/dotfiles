Proofread the document at $ARGUMENTS for journal submission. Run these checks systematically:

## Language & Style
- No em dashes or en dashes anywhere. Replace with commas, periods, colons, or parentheses.
- Consistent spelling (pick one: British or American English, match what the paper uses).
- No passive voice where active is clearer.
- No weasel words ("somewhat", "relatively", "fairly").
- Tighten every sentence. Cut filler.

## Academic Rigor
- All figure/table references in text exist and vice versa (no orphaned references).
- Numbers cited in text match corresponding table cells exactly.
- All citations in text appear in the bibliography and vice versa.
- Variable names and abbreviations are defined on first use and used consistently.
- Equations are numbered and referenced correctly.

## Formatting
- Consistent heading hierarchy and numbering.
- Table formatting: aligned columns, consistent decimal places, proper units.
- Figure captions are descriptive and standalone.

## Process
1. Read the entire document first.
2. Report ALL issues found, grouped by category above.
3. Fix each issue, showing the before/after.
4. Re-scan after fixes to catch anything introduced by edits.
5. Report a clean summary when done.

If a language_tweak.txt or style guide file exists in the project, follow it exactly. Do NOT apply your own preferences over explicit instructions.
